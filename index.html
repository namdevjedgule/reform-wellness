Vitamin Deficiency

Micronutrient Deficiency Indian Women: Iron, B12 & Vitamin D Explained

Many women in India are not receiving the nutrients they require. This is causing problems with energy, immunity, bone health and overall well-being. Women of all ages from teenagers to adults are affected. They may feel tired all the time, experience hair fall, or get sick easily.

The common problems are iron deficiency, vitamin B12 deficiency, vitamin D deficiency and calcium deficiency. Women who live in cities like Pune and Kharadi are more likely to have these problems because they often eat irregular meals and do not get enough sunlight.

Iron deficiency is the most common nutritional deficiency found in India. When iron levels are low, the body cannot function properly red blood cells cannot carry enough oxygen, leaving you feeling weak and exhausted.

Another major issue is vitamin B12 deficiency. Vitamin B12 is essential for healthy brain function, nerve health, and energy production. Because B12 is largely found in animal products, vegans and vegetarians face a much higher risk of deficiency.
